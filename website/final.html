Escape Room

CSC 377 Spring 2020, Selynna Sun

Intro

I'm a huge fan of escape rooms - they're incredibly immersive in real life and contain so many interesting puzzles and riddles. Due to ongoing circumstances, we unfortunately can't go to escape rooms irl. what better way to simulate one than by making one in VR?

Puzzles

This escape room contains three separate stages: Hidden Switches, Rube Goldberg, and Travel Trivia. I don't want to spoil the puzzles, so all of the following information has spoiler tags. Hover to uncover the solution.

1. Hidden Switches

The objective of this room is to find two switches to unlock the door to the next room.
Switch 1: underneath the table
Switch 2:
solve the riddle. door on the left leads to inferno, door on the right leads to lion that hasn't eaten in three months. you open the right door because the lion is (realistically) dead after not eating for so long. this means you choose the right painting, which has a switch behind it.

2. Rube Goldberg

The objective of this room is to use the rube goldberg machine to trigger the switch with the ball in the room.

3. Travel Trivia

The objective of this room is to get a 4 digit code . This is by far the hardest room.
Hint 1:
The first picture on the wall shows the order of which the digits go. You must solve all the puzzles but combine the digits in that order.
Hint 2:
The first picture is of the Great Wall. This is located in China. The capital of Beijing is China, so the digit for this puzzle is 7.
Hint 3:
The second picture is of a chess board. The objective is to put the king into checkmate in 1 move. The move is e7#, add 4 to the last number to get e11, and locate the result on the world map on the wall. You get a square that contains Italy, which has a capital of Rome, so the digit is 4.
Hint 4:
The third picture is of oranges. There's a table of 17 oranges, and because the vitamin most commonly associated with oranges is vitamin c, you get c17. That square on the world map contains Russia, which has a capital of Moscow, so the resulting digit is 6.
Hint 5:
The fourth picture shows a picture of a puzzle. There's a 4-piece puzzle on the table, and when you rearrange them, you get the Indonesian flag. The capital is Jakarta, which means the digit is 7.
